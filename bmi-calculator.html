Maintaining a Healthy Weight

Balanced Nutrition

Focus on whole foods, fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and whole grains. Limit processed foods and sugars.

Regular Exercise

Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity each week.

Quality Sleep

Adults need 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night. Poor sleep can affect hormones related to appetite.

Understanding BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a simple index of weight-for-height that is commonly used to classify underweight, overweight, and obesity in adults.

BMI is calculated by dividing a person's weight in kilograms by the square of their height in meters. The formula is:

BMI = weight (kg) / [height (m)]²

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it has limitations:

  • It may overestimate body fat in athletes and those with muscular builds
  • It may underestimate body fat in older persons and those who have lost muscle
  • It doesn't account for differences in body composition or fat distribution

BMI should be used as a starting point for understanding weight-related health risks. For a comprehensive health assessment, consult a healthcare provider.
